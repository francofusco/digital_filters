Computes the numerator and denominator of an exponential filter.

The exponential filter is defined by the following difference equation:

\[ y_k = \alpha x_k + (1-\alpha) y_{k-1} \]

This function computes the numerator and denominator of the corresponding transfer function.

Computes the numerator and denominator of an exponential filter.

The exponential filter is defined by the following difference equation:

\[ y_k = \alpha x_k + (1-\alpha) y_{k-1} \]

wherein \( \alpha = 1 - e^{-\frac{\Delta t}{\tau}} \), with \(\Delta t\) being the sampling time and \(\tau\) the time constant of the filter. The output will reach respectively the 63%, 86% and 95% at the times \(\tau\), \(2\tau\) and \(3\tau\).

Parameters
[in]samplingsampling time \(\Delta t\).
[in]time_constanttime constant \(\tau\).
[out]numeratornumerator of the transfer function.
[out]denominatordenominator of the transfer function.
